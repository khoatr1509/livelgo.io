Livel

A better online traveling experience

Livel is a product which is built while we participate in Solution Challenge 2021 - a global competition by Google. Here our main goal is to attempt to solve 17 SDGs - Sustainable Development Goals of Unesco. These goals are a call for action by all countries to promote prosperity while protecting the planet until 2030.

Diving into problem

Traveling has become an important demand in everyone’s life. The touring industry has been considered as a potential developing industry in many countries.

Since Covid 19 pandemic, the tourism industry has been facing serious problems and 2020 witnessed a major decline in the number of international trips. Touring companies now are facing a serious problem of reaching customers as touring restrictions. As a result, this leads to the loss of the economy and GDP.

For the nine months of 2020, international visitors arrive at our country reached 3,788.5 thousand, down 70.6% over to the same period last year, of which 97,3% were international visitors in the first quarter of 2020, the second quarter and third quarter only accounted for 1.5% and 1.2%.

Solution

For these reasons and motivation, we came up with an idea that develops a commercial mobile application that allows tourists to book and go on an online tour with their favorite guides. We expect to create a new type of traveling not only to expand the tourists market but also promote the traditional touring services. Furthermore, these activities will enhance the image of countries and spread it all over the world by means of cutting-edge technologies.

Target user

Here, we are focusing on two types of users: the smartphone user aging from 18-40 and the licensed tour guides. People from 18-40 years old use smartphones most frequently and they have a stable income. Meanwhile, there are 7 types of tour guides working at the touring destination with different. Tourists will able and to travel on the trip that the tour guide create via Livel application

Design Process

Start day: 25 January 2021

Here we apply and base on Design Thinking Process. The design thinking process includes 5 steps in a product design: Define, Empathy, Ideate, Prototype and Test.

Insights of traveling habits

In order to know more about our general scenarios of the customer on a large scale, we do 2 types of research: quantitative and qualitative research. We took a survey of 20 tourists whose ages range from 20 to 26 years old. Our goals were to confirm our hypothesis about the habit of traveling, their frequency of traveling, and what factors affect their decision in choosing a place for traveling. We also created a focus group for further survey and testing our prototype.
